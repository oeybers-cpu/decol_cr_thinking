Critical Reflection: Decoloniality

Foundational Text

Before answering the reflection questions, please read the foundational article:

Required Reading

Decolonising Education in Africa: What does it mean? (Ndlovu-Gatsheni, 2022)

Reflective Questions

After reading the article, reflect on the following questions thoughtfully:

Question 1: Positionality & Understanding

How does your own background, identity, and educational experiences shape your understanding of decoloniality? In what ways does the concept of "coloniality of knowledge" challenge or confirm your previous understanding of what counts as valid knowledge in academic settings?

Question 2: Discipline & Application

How does your academic discipline approach questions of knowledge production, power, and social justice? How might a decolonial perspective enhance or challenge the conventions in your field? What does "re-humanising" education mean to you, and how could it be practically implemented in your academic context?

Question 3: Synthesis & Evolution

Having considered the principles of decoloniality (re-humanising, pluriversalising, deracialising, decolonising the mind) and reflected on your discipline and positionality: What is your key takeaway from this session? How has your understanding of decoloniality and its implications for education evolved, and how might this influence your approach to learning and knowledge?
